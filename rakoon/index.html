Powerlifting Metrics

Real formulas. Real science.

All calculations are based on peer-reviewed research and official federation standards. We show you exactly how every metric is calculated — no black boxes.

e1RM

Estimated 1RM

Predicts your one-rep max from submaximal lifts using the Epley formula.

Weight × (1 + Reps ÷ 30)
DOTS

DOTS Score

Modern bodyweight-adjusted score. More accurate than Wilks at extreme weight classes.

4th degree polynomial
WLK

Wilks Score

Classic relative strength score. Compare yourself across weight classes.

2020 updated coefficients
IPF

IPF GL Points

Official IPF competition scoring. Updated every 4 years based on competition data.

Exponential function
S/B/D

Lift Ratios

Compare your squat, bench, and deadlift to identify weaknesses.

Target: S 90%, B 62%
VOL

Training Volume

Track tonnage for progressive overload. The key metric for strength gains.

Sets × Reps × Weight

Sources: IPF Technical Rules, OpenPowerlifting.org, Strength Level, NSCA Research
